# Carelane visual system

## Identity

Carelane combines humane care with operational control. The design must carry both ideas at once: warm enough to feel human, disciplined enough to feel dependable.

## Typography

Preferred UI stack:

`Inter, ui-sans-serif, system-ui, -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "Segoe UI", sans-serif`

Optional editorial accent for a limited number of large headings:

`"Source Serif 4", Georgia, serif`

Do not depend on external fonts for layout stability. Use local/system fallbacks.

### Scale

- Hero: 56/64 desktop, 42/48 tablet, 36/42 mobile
- Section H2: 36/44 desktop, 30/38 tablet, 28/34 mobile
- Subsection H3: 24/32
- Lead: 18/28
- Body: 16/24
- Small: 14/20
- Overline: 12/16, 0.08em tracking

## Layout

- Maximum content width: 1280 px
- Desktop page padding: 48 px
- Tablet page padding: 32 px
- Mobile page padding: 20 px
- Use a 12-column grid on desktop
- Section vertical padding: 96 to 128 px desktop, 64 to 80 px tablet, 48 to 64 px mobile

## Surface hierarchy

- Canvas: deep navy, never pure black
- Surface 1: broad section panels
- Surface 2: content cards and product frames
- Surface 3: focused or elevated operational elements
- Use borders sparingly. Prefer tonal separation, shadow and whitespace.

## Accent discipline

- Violet: brand, primary CTA, focus and selected state
- Blue: information and system intelligence
- Teal/green: completed or confirmed
- Amber: pending, constrained or attention
- Red: blocked or critical only

Never use all accents at equal intensity in one section.

## Shape language

- Cards: 16 to 24 px radius
- Controls: 10 to 14 px radius
- Pills: only for small statuses, filters and compact metadata
- Avoid nested rounded rectangles that create a “card farm”

## Iconography

Use one consistent outline icon family, 1.75 to 2 px stroke. Icons should describe:
- people and organisations;
- workflow and routing;
- decisions and accountability;
- security and access;
- capacity and time.

Avoid decorative sparkles as feature icons except for the brand mark.
